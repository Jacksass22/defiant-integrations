            <h2 class="section-title">Breaking Out of Pilot Purgatory</h2>

            <p>One of the biggest pitfalls in corporate AI programs is getting stuck in "pilot purgatory." Companies launch numerous pilot projects and proof-of-concepts but few ever graduate to full-scale deployment.</p>

            <div class="chart-container">
                <div class="chart-title">The Pilot Purgatory Problem</div>

            <p>According to IDC research, only about 12% of AI proof-of-concepts ever progress to widespread production useâ€”meaning a staggering 88% never make it out of the pilot stage. This creates what I call "operational entropy"â€”resources and momentum dissipating across too many uncoordinated experiments.</p>

                    <li>Treating AI pilots as isolated tech experiments without business integration planning</li>
                    <li>Unclear objectives and insufficient data readiness</li>
                    <li>Lack of in-house expertise to scale successful pilots</li>
                    <li>Over-enthusiasm for trying the latest AI tools without strategic focus</li>
                    <li>Developing AI solutions in departmental silos</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
